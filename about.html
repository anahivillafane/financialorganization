What is the Financial Organization?

"Financial organization" is a broad term that refers to entities, institutions or companies that provide financial services or manage monetary resources. These organizations may include commercial banks, credit unions, investment funds, insurance companies, government financial agencies, among others. .

Main functions of a financial organization:

Asset Management: Provides services to manage and grow clients' assets, such as investment funds, portfolio management, etc.

Payment Services: Facilitates financial transactions like electronic transfers, issuance of credit/debit cards, payment processing, among others.

Financial Advisory: Provides guidance and advice on investments, financial planning, debt management, and other financial aspects.

Insurance and Risk Management: Offers products and services to protect against financial risks, including life, health, property, and accident insurance.
